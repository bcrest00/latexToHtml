8.9   Theorems et al.

Definition 16 (right-angled triangles). A right-angled triangle is a triangle whose sides of length a, b and c, in some permutation of order, satisfies a2 + b2 = c2.

Lemma 17. The triangle with sides of length 3, 4 and 5 is right-angled.

This lemma follows from the Definition 16 as 32 + 42 = 9 + 16 = 25 = 52.

Theorem 18 (Pythagorean triplets). Triangles with sides of length a = p2 − q2, b = 2pq and c = p2 + q2 are right-angled triangles.

Prove this Theorem 18 by the algebra a2 +b2 = (p2 −q2)2 +(2pq)2 = p4 −2p2q2 +q4 +4p2q2 = p4 +2p2q2 +q4 = (p2 +q2)2 = c2.
